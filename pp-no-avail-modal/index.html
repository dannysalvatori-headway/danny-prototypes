Provider Profile — No-Avail Modal Prototype

SEM ad → zero-availability provider profile. Three modal variants for redirecting visitors to available providers.
Context: 42% of PMax traffic lands on zero-avail profiles (0.93% book rate, $597/booking). 337 self-recoverers navigated 15 pages avg to find an available provider.

Variant
Control. Current no-avail experience for SEM ad → profile. No hurricane banner. Desktop: “Book session” header with CantBookCard. Mobile: sticky footer with “See other providers.” No modal, no redirect.
B
Smart Redirect Modal. On page load (500ms–1s delay), show modal with 3 curated similar providers who have open appointments. “See all available providers like Dr. Milsap” primary CTA. Dismiss option to continue reading. Replicates CA rematch mechanism (52% of bookers ended up with a different provider).
C
Three Options Modal. Structured choices mapping to Pod Plan mindsets: (1) See alternatives → Leaning No, (2) Painted-door waitlist signal → Undecided, (3) Continue reading → Leaning Yes. Waitlist is demand signal only — no email capture, no CRM. Tests whether explicit choice outperforms a single-path redirect.
D
Insurance-First Modal. Leads with insurance verification before showing alternatives. Addresses #1 system-caused blocker (36% of match-found-no-booking when provider IS available). Confirms insurance → shows filtered count → shows 3 providers. Higher-quality redirect, potentially lower modal completion rate.
